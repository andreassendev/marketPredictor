# Denmark Parliamentary Election - March 24, 2026

## Election Overview

Denmark holds parliamentary elections on March 24, 2026. Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen called the vote more than six months early, amid a revival in support for her Social Democrats following the conflict with US President Trump over Greenland.

The election threatens to upend Denmark's postwar political stability. Emerging centrist and right-wing forces could break the longstanding left-right bloc scheme. A new majority alliance spanning traditional divides seems possible, signaling major policy shifts.

## Key Campaign Issues

1. **Greenland/Trump crisis** - Boosted Frederiksen's popularity initially, but effect fading
2. **Housing affordability** - Prices nearly tripled in Copenhagen over 30 years
3. **Rural vs urban divide** - Young people leaving smaller towns
4. **Defense spending** - Military conscription expansion proposed
5. **Cost of living** - Fuel prices straining households
6. **Immigration** - Danmarksdemokraterne and Dansk Folkeparti pushing stricter controls

## Scandals & Controversies

- **Liberal Alliance leader Alex Vanopslagh** admitted to past cocaine use while leading the party. One candidate was expelled from party lists. This created significant internal party turmoil.
- Despite scandal, Liberal Alliance polls remain stable at ~11%
- Vanopslagh has not ruled out nominating himself as PM candidate

## Coalition Dynamics

- The incumbent three-party coalition (Socialdemokraterne + Venstre + Moderaterne) is expected to lose its majority
- Red bloc is close to majority WITHOUT Moderaterne
- A centrist coalition spanning traditional left-right divides is possible
- Interest in centrist government stronger among Venstre supporters than Social Democrats
- Danmarksdemokraterne (led by Inger Støjberg) has become a right-wing powerhouse with strict immigration + rural representation message
