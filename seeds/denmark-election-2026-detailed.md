## 3. KEY ACTORS — Detailed Profiles

### Mette Frederiksen (Socialdemokraterne) — PM, Red Bloc Leader
- Age 48, PM since 2019. Experienced crisis manager.
- Called early election to capitalize on Greenland/Trump boost
- Enacted one-off tax-free payment of DKK 2,500-5,000 to ~2 million households
- Under internal criticism for first time in a decade after poor EU/local elections
- Wants "red bloc reunion" with SF — signaling wealth tax and pension reform
- Communication style: Authoritative, empathetic, occasionally combative

### Pia Olsen Dyhr (SF) — Red Bloc #2, Climate Champion
- Leading SF to historic highs (~13-15%)
- Core issues: Climate, education, public school reform
- Party positioned as traditional social democrats + green politics
- Key to any red bloc government
- Communication style: Passionate, policy-focused, relatable

### Alex Vanopslagh (Liberal Alliance) — Blue Bloc's Rising Star (Damaged?)
- Young, charismatic libertarian leader
- COCAINE SCANDAL (March 16): Admitted to cocaine use while party leader
- Quote: "It happened once or twice at most in a party setting"
- Had been seen as PM candidate for blue bloc
- Analyst Noa Redington: "This can give the image of Liberal Alliance as people who conduct their politics like students. That's unfortunate in an election campaign that was supposed to elevate Vanopslagh as a prime ministerial candidate."
- One candidate expelled from party lists due to scandal fallout
- Despite scandal, polls remain relatively stable at ~11%
- Used creative Tinder-style election poster to attract young voters
- Communication style: Sharp, witty, provocative, social media savvy

### Troels Lund Poulsen (Venstre) — Blue Bloc PM Candidate
- Venstre leader and current defence minister
- On Feb 26 declared himself available to lead blue bloc government
- Endorsed by Conservatives and Dansk Folkeparti as preferred PM candidate
- Party declining in polls (-1.5 in 30 days)
- Communication style: Steady, institutional, traditional politician

### Inger Støjberg (Danmarksdemokraterne) — Right-Wing Populist
- Founded party after leaving Venstre
- Core message: Strict immigration control + rural representation
- "Resonating widely" according to analysts
- Polling stable at ~8%
- Communication style: Direct, populist, anti-establishment

### Morten Messerschmidt (Dansk Folkeparti) — Resurgent Nationalist
- Transformed DP into "media-savvy movement targeting disenchanted blue-collar voters tired of high living costs"
- Biggest gain of any party: +5.1% from 2022
- Communication style: Provocative, media-savvy, appeals to working class

### Lars Løkke Rasmussen (Moderaterne) — The Kingmaker
- Former PM, leads centrist party
- 6.9% support = DECISIVE for government formation
- Red bloc cannot govern without him (86 seats, need 90)
- Interest in centrist coalition stronger among Venstre supporters than Social Democrats
- Communication style: Experienced, pragmatic, deal-maker

## 4. KEY CAMPAIGN ISSUES (ranked by voter impact)

### A. Cost of Living Crisis
- Housing prices nearly tripled in Copenhagen over 30 years
- Fuel prices straining households
- Frederiksen's one-off payment seen as election sweetener
- Blue bloc proposes lower fuel taxes

### B. Immigration & Integration
- Danmarksdemokraterne and DP pushing stricter controls
- Social Democrats have also moved right on immigration
- Key wedge issue between red and blue blocs

### C. Greenland/Trump Crisis
- Trump's demands over Greenland initially boosted Frederiksen
- BUT in the televised party-leader debate when election was announced, Greenland was NOT mentioned once
- Effect appears to be fading

### D. Defense & Military
- Venstre proposes doubling conscripts
- Defense spending debate linked to NATO commitments
- Nuclear weapons deployment discussions

### E. Climate & Environment
- SF's strongest issue
- Pig farming welfare standards debate
- Drinking water quality issues in 60 municipalities

### F. Rural vs Urban Divide
- Young people leaving smaller towns
- Danmarksdemokraterne capitalizing on rural frustration
- Housing affordability affects both urban and rural voters differently

## 5. THE 2ND PLACE RACE (Polymarket Focus)

**Polymarket prices SF at 73% to finish 2nd.**

### The contenders:
1. **SF (12.9%)** — Strong momentum, consistent polling lead for 2nd
2. **Liberal Alliance (10.9%)** — Cocaine scandal may hurt, but stable so far
3. **Venstre (9.8%)** — Declining, unlikely to overtake

### Arguments FOR SF finishing 2nd (supporting 73%):
- Consistent 2-4 point lead over Liberal Alliance across all polls
- Gaining momentum (+0.5 in 30 days)
- Climate and welfare issues resonate with Danish voters
- Red bloc overall strengthening
- Historical: SF tends to outperform polls when red bloc is strong

### Arguments AGAINST SF / FOR Liberal Alliance upset:
- Vanopslagh scandal may have paradoxical "all publicity is good publicity" effect
- Liberal Alliance appeals to young, first-time voters who are underrepresented in polls
- SF's poll ceiling may already be reached
- If Moderaterne voters shift, they may go to Liberal Alliance over SF
- Cocaine scandal created massive media attention = name recognition boost

### Arguments FOR Venstre dark horse:
- Declared PM candidacy may rally blue bloc voters
- Conservative and DP endorsement could create "strategic voting" effect
- BUT: -1.5 point decline makes this unlikely
